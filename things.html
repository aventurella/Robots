Fun Things to do in Santa Fe (besides going to 'the plaza')

  • Ten Thousand Waves

    Not only did Danica work here for years, it is truly one of the most amazing places in Santa Fe. We recommend at least booking an outdoor tub for an hour - Waterfall & Shoji are two of our favorites.

    http://www.tenthousandwaves.com/
  • Santa Fe Brewing Company

    This was the beer-a-mid we created while taste testing for the wedding. We suggest you create your own.

    http://www.santafebrewing.com/
  • 2nd Street Brewery

    Good atmosphere, Good Beer.

    http://www.secondstreetbrewery.com/
  • The Screen

    Check out a movie at Adam & Danica's alma mater. Great selection of independent/foreign/hard to find films and we aren't just saying that because we both used to work there ;)

    http://thescreensf.com/
  • Canyon Road

    While you're eating at one of our recommended Canyon Road establishments it wouldn't hurt to take a stroll and check out all the art.

    http://www.canyonroadarts.com/
  • Shidoni

    It doesn't get more Santa Fe than Shidoni.

    http://www.shidoni.com/
  • Santa Fe Ski Basin

    Take a Hike! Beautiful views and plenty of trails. This will make you out-of-towners re-think the Southwest.

    http://www.skisantafe.com/
  • Flea Market/Santa Fe Opera

    If you can't find something at this open-air flea market, it may not exist on planet earth. Then again, this is Santa Fe, so you may not find things from planet earth. The opera house is also beautiful.

    http://www.pueblooftesuquefleamarket.com/
    http://www.santafeopera.org/

Day Trips that we would actually take

  • Bandelier National Monument

    Not great for anyone that is scared of heights but a really perfect way to spend the day. History, nature & adventure.

    http://www.nps.gov/band/
  • Madrid, New Mexico

    The coal mine tour is actually pretty dang fun. They have a great tavern and tons of fun shops.

    http://visitmadridnm.com/
  • Ojo Caliente Mineral Springs

    Nothing better than relaxing in natural mineral springs. All types of springs from Iron to Lithia and Arsenic. They also have a mud pool!

    http://ojospa.com/
  • Jemez Springs, New Mexico

    The little town is fun, good restaurants and great natural hot springs. Our FAVORITE thing to do is the East Fork Trail. We will be taking Lucy there for her very first water adventure before the big day. Your feet will get wet on this trial.

    http://www.jemezsprings.org/
    http://networks.cs.ucdavis.edu/~amitabha/LosAlamosHiking/
  • Los Alamos, New Mexico

    Ummm, they only built the atomic bomb here. One of Adam's favorite things in Los Alamos is The Black Hole. Lots of cool stuff there.

    http://www.roadsideamerica.com/story/18130
  • Santa Fe Railroad Tours

    We have actually done this a few separate times and it's really fun. I love getting to drink some whiskey on a 1930's era railroad car and for my 23rd Birthday we (Adam, Matt, Zoe, Meredith and I) rode our bikes out to Lamy and caught the train back to town. Good times!

    http://www.santafetours.net/
  • VLA (Very Large Array)

    Find some aliens, they found 'em in the movie Contact with Jodi Foster right here at the VLA. SCIENCE! *shaaaaa*

    http://www.vla.nrao.edu/
    http://www.nrao.edu/index.php/learn/vlavc
  • Tent Rocks

    We went here for one of our field trips in college — Natural Disasters. If you have the time, it's an amazing place to go and very photographic.

    http://www.blm.gov/nm/st/en/prog/recreation/rio_puerco/kasha_katuwe_tent_rocks.html
    http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kasha-Katuwe_Tent_Rocks_National_Monument
